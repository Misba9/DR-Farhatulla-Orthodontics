Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do you take my insurance?
A: We would be able to check on your orthodontic benefits for you and discuss your coverage and braces pricing during the video chat. Read about how our office works with your orthodontic insurance.

The most commonly used insurance carriers by our patients are Aetna, Cigna, Delta, Guardian, Horizon, and Metlife.

Note: benefits from Medicaid, HMO, DMO, and medical plans are not accepted.

Q: How much do braces cost?
A: If treatment is recommended, then we can talk about getting braces and the investment involved. For any portion of treatment your insurance doesn’t cover, of online consultation available. Read an overview of the cost of braces at our office.

Q: How much does the first visit cost?
A: You are not charged for the 10-minute consultation.

Q: How long is the first visit?
A: Please allow 10 minutes for the first visit.

Q: What happens afterwards?
A: The second visit is a records appointment.

After you ask any questions about treatment and discuss finances, you will schedule a 20 minute visit for detailed records. The records are studied by Dr. Farhatulla to plan placement of braces, clear aligners, expanders, and retainers.

Q: Will I get X-rays at my first visit?
A: Panoramic and Cephalometric X-rays are captured in-office at a separate records visit.

Q: Is the records visit refundable?
A: No. The records visit is not refundable.

Q: I just want to know how long treatment will take and how much it will cost. Can you tell me the cost over the phone?
A: You can read about our pricing for braces and how we work with your orthodontic monthly insurance. If you would like specific numbers, please book an in-office exam to talk about the length of treatment and investment involved.

Q: Is my child too young?
A: We help patients ages 7 to 50.

Q: What if there are still baby teeth?
A: You would want to book an in-office exam before all the baby teeth fall out because orthodontic problems begin when baby teeth are still present.
